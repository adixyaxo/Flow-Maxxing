THE LORE

WHY THIS
EXISTS.

TaskMaxxing started as a personal frustration. Every productivity app felt like a corporate spreadsheet or a minimalist puzzle you needed a PhD to solve.

AD

Aditya Dagar

Founder & Developer

GitHub Twitter

I spent more time organizing my tasks than actually doing them. I'd download a new app, spend two days setting up a complex system of tags, folders, and priority matrices, and then completely abandon it by Thursday.

It felt like these apps were built for project managers at Fortune 500 companies, not for people who just wanted to get things done without having to fill out a form every time they had an idea.

"I wanted something that made productivity feel rewarding, not like another chore. Something brutal, fast, and completely unhinged."

So I built TaskMaxxing. I stripped away the fluff, the motivational quotes, and the endless sub-menus. What's left is a tool for locking in. It connects your fleeting thoughts to your main objectives and tracks your aura points along the way.

If you're tired of switching tools and just want to finish your work, you're in the right place.
